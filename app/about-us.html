About us / Journey

Our team of ex-bankers, technologists, and product designers started working in Decentralized Finance (DeFi) almost two years ago. Our first products were LoanScan, the leading analytics portal for loans issued on the Ethereum blockchain, and Bloqboard, a lending platform.

We are launching the Linen App now because we saw a knowledge gap between the tech and finance savvy who earn high yield on their cash using stablecoins and the others who do not know how to use blockchain-based technologies. This provided us an opportunity to create new products, such as Linen Cash, which is a high-yield cash management service powered by lending liquidity pools on the Compound protocol, and the Linen Card, a debit card to spend Linen Cash. Our products allow the average person to take advantage of these new possibilities without being directly exposed to the volatile cryptocurrency.

We have raised funding from several leading venture investors, and now we want to provide equity to our members so that they can benefit from our success, too.

You, our community, are the key element for why we exist.

Linen Cash is a cash management service that allows U.S. residents of certain states to supply funds into a Compound liquidity pool. Linen Cash is developed by Linen Mobile, Inc. Linen Mobile, Inc. is not a bank and the cash or digital cash (USDC) you deposit using Linen mobile app will not be stored at a bank. Linen Mobile, Inc. provides software to access Compound Liquidity pools. Funds supplied to Compound liquidity pools using Linen software are not insured by the Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation (FDIC) or any other government agency. You could lose money accessing liquidity pools using Linen mobile app.

You could lose money by accessing yields through the Linen mobile app. Although the Compound liquidity pool that the Linen mobile app provides access to is overcollateralized and is monitored by third parties for liquidations in real-time, neither Linen nor Compound cannot guarantee it will do so. Accessing Compound liquidity pools is not insured or guaranteed by the Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation (FDIC) or any other government agency. Compound Labs, Inc., the developer of Compound liquidity pools, has no legal obligation to provide software development support to its liquidity pools and you should not expect that Compound Labs, Inc. will provide software development to its liquidity pools at any time. It is possible that Compound liquidity pools will not have enough liquidity at a time of withdrawal if the borrowing demand becomes high.

Linen Mobile, Inc. does not charge transaction or debit card fees. Please refer to Card Agreement and banking services provider agreements when become available.
